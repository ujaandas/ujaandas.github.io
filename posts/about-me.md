I'm currently a postgraduate student in Computer Science at the
University of Warwick, where I'm writing my dissertation on the formal
verification of structured LLM outputs. 

![Warwick](/static/warwick.jpg)

Before this, I completed my BEng in Computer Engineering back home at 
HKUST, and spent a semester abroad at Northwestern University.

![HKUST](/static/hkust.jpg)

A little bit about me: I was born in Kolkata, India, but moved to Hong Kong
when I was 3. I've always been into tech, gaming and
coding (does [Scratch](https://scratch.mit.edu/users/dasu1) count?).

Professionally, I've had a mix of experiences at various startups and research labs,
focusing on backend and systems programming. I've designed compilers and DSLs for agents, 
built static analysis pipelines that process millions and millions of lines of code, and 
worked on all sorts of high-performance backend systems - from geo-spatial infrastructure 
to agent-driven workflows.

Thanks to my undergrad in Computer Engineering, I also got to play around with
embedded systems and robotics - our final year project was an autonomous
person-following robot!!!

![robot](/static/robot.jpg)

I'm also a published author at some of the biggest HCI journals and
(soon-to-be) software engineering ones! 

Outside of work and research, I spend time on my hobby-projects, contributing to 
open-source, writing on my blog (duh), and self-hosting! I'm a big fan of Nix, 
and am very interested in compilers and programming languages.

I love cooking, League of Legends (I'm Bronze II), and simracing, but none of
those as much as my dog!
